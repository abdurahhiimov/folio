The problem

Your LinkedIn profile is not enough.
Not anymore.

Hiring managers Google you before they call you. What they find — or don't find — shapes their opinion before you've said a single word. A LinkedIn page everyone has. A well-crafted personal website that tells your actual story? Almost nobody has that.

It signals that you take your career seriously. That you're someone who invests in their professional identity the way serious professionals do.

93%
of employers say a strong online presence positively influences their hiring decisions — and a personal website is the most credible form of that presence.

You're harder to find than you think

Search your own name. If nothing comes up, or if it's not what you'd want a future employer to see, that's a problem worth fixing.

A resume tells them what. A website tells them who.

Your values, your origin story, your thesis on the work — none of that fits in a two-page PDF. A website is the only place you fully control your narrative.

The DIY trap: time you don't have

Website builders promise ease and deliver hours of frustration. The result usually looks like exactly what it is — someone who built their own site on a Sunday afternoon.

Generic templates look generic

If your site looks like everyone else's, it signals exactly the same thing your application does — that you're one of many. Folio builds around your specific story, not a starting point.
